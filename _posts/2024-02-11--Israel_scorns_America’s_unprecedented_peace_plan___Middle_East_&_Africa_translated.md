# 以色列无视美国前所未有的和平方案|中东与非洲


# Israel scorns America’s unprecedented peace plan | Middle East & Africa

![image](https://images.weserv.nl/?url=www.economist.com/img/b/1280/720/90/media-assets/image/20240210_MAP504.jpg)

<div></div><p><span>I</span><small>srael’s triumpH</small> in the Six Day War of 1967 was met by the “three nos” at an Arab summit in Khartoum: no peace with Israel, no recognition, no negotiations. The <a href="https://www.economist.com/israel-hamas">war in Gaza</a> seems to be having the opposite effect, American officials say. Saudi Arabia, the most important Arab state, is saying yes to peace, negotiations and recognition of the Jewish state—if Israel agrees to a “clear and credible pathway” to the creation of a Palestinian state in the <a href="https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2023/12/07/israel-is-strangling-the-west-banks-economy">West Bank</a> and Gaza Strip, which it occupied in 1967. There may be two more yeses on offer: yes to Arab security assurances to Israel, beyond diplomatic relations; and yes to Arab states’ help with reforming the Palestinian Authority (<small>pa</small>) so it is fit to run Gaza.</p>

以色列在1967年六日战争中的胜利，却在开罗的阿拉伯首脑会议上遇到了“三个不”回应：不与以色列和平、不承认、不谈判。美国官员表示，加沙地带的冲突似乎产生了相反的效果。

最重要的阿拉伯国家沙特阿拉伯表示，如果以色列同意建立一个明确且可信的途径，通往西岸（巴勒斯坦人1967年占领的领土）和加沙地带（同上）创建巴勒斯坦国，那么它愿意接受和平、谈判和承认犹太国家。

可能还有其他两个积极回应：阿拉伯国家向以色列提供超出外交关系范围的安全保证；以及阿拉伯国家帮助改革巴勒斯坦权力机构（PA），使其能够管理加沙地带。

But to judge from the scornful reaction of Israel’s prime minister, Binyamin Netanyahu, Israel is now the naysayer.</p>

本周，美国国务卿 Antony Blinken 在阿拉伯半岛穿梭（这是他上任后第五次区域访问）——自10月7日哈马斯袭击以来。但根据以色列总理本杰明·内塔尼亚胡的不屑反应来看，现在可能是以色列在唱反调了。


<aside><p><a href="https://www.economist.com/israel-hamas"><i>Read all our coverage of the war between Israel and Hamas</i></a></p></aside><p>The outlook in the Middle East seems dire. Iran’s allies in Lebanon are exchanging regular fire with Israel; and those in Syria, Iraq and Yemen have been attacking American forces, provoking rounds of retaliation, before and during his trip (see next story). More than 27,000 Palestinians are reported dead in Gaza. Most of the territory’s population is displaced and facing disease and hunger. Israel <a href="https://www.economist.com/leaders/2024/01/18/charging-israel-with-genocide-makes-a-mockery-of-the-icj">stands accused of genocide</a> in the International Court of Justice. In the eyes of many, America’s reputation has also been stained by President Joe Biden’s support for Israel.</p>

中东的前景看起来黯淡。黎巴嫩的伊朗盟友与以色列频繁交火；而叙利亚、伊拉克和也门的那些人则在攻击美国军队，引发了在他行程前后（见下一则新闻）的一轮又一轮的报复行动。据报道，超过27,000名巴勒斯坦人在加沙死亡。该地区大部分人口流离失所，面临疾病和饥饿。以色列被国际刑事法院指控犯有种族屠杀罪。在许多人看来，美国的声誉也因为总统乔·拜登对以色列的支持而受到玷污。


<div><div><div id="econ-1"></div></div></div><p>Yet Mr Blinken is seeking to turn the horror of Gaza into a chance for peace. And American officials seemed elated by their talks with Saudi Arabia’s crown prince and de facto ruler, Muhammad bin Salman.</p>

然而，布林肯正试图将加沙的恐怖转化为和平的机会。美国官员似乎对与沙特阿拉伯王储、事实上的统治者穆罕默德·本·萨勒曼的会谈感到欣喜。


<p>Once treated as a “pariah”, in the past words of Mr Biden, Saudi Arabia has become central to America’s ambitious diplomatic strategy. This involves securing an “extended” pause in the fighting in Gaza with a hostage and prisoner exchange, followed by a permanent ceasefire, Israeli acceptance of a Palestinian state, Saudi Arabia’s recognition of Israel and new American security commitments. Mr Blinken seems convinced that, rather than 1967, the moment in Israel today is more akin to the aftermath of the Arab-Israeli war of 1973 and the Palestinian intifada (uprising) of 1987-93. Then, the pain of conflict led to the peace treaty with Egypt in 1979 and the Oslo accords of 1993 that created the <small>pa</small>.</p>

经过过去拜登所说的话中所描述的“边缘化”时期，沙特阿拉伯已经成为美国雄心勃勃外交战略的核心。这一策略包括确保加沙地带冲突的“延长”暂停，通过人质和囚犯交换，随后实现永久停火，以色列接受巴勒斯坦国家，沙特阿拉伯对以色列的认可以及新的美国安全承诺。

布林肯先生似乎深信不疑，今天的以色列并不是1967年的复刻，更像是1973年阿以战争后的余波，以及1987-93年间爆发的巴勒斯坦起义（intifada）。那时候，冲突带来的痛苦促成了1979年与埃及签署的和平条约，以及1993年创建巴勒斯坦自治政府的奥斯陆协议。


<p>Even so, the path to a regional deal is far from assured. The hostage deal—the essential first step in America’s plan—rests on a man whom the Israelis are determined to kill: Yahya Sinwar, the leader of Hamas in Gaza. He is thought to be hiding with hostages in tunnels under Gaza.</p>

即便如此，中东地区协议的达成之路仍然充满不确定。人质交换——这是美国计划中的首要步骤——依赖于一个以色列人决心要杀死的人：加沙地带哈马斯组织的领导人叶海亚·辛瓦尔（Yahya Sinwar）。据信，他藏在加沙地下的隧道中与人质在一起。


<p>That said, Mr Blinken brought hopeful news. On February 6th the emir of Qatar told him Hamas had responded to a hostage deal drafted by Israel, America, Egypt and Qatar. The answer was deemed “positive” by Qatar and promising by Mr Blinken (albeit with some “non-starters”). Yet Mr Netanyahu dismissed it as “delusional”. </p>

话虽如此，但布林肯先生带来了令人鼓舞的消息。2月6日，卡塔尔埃米尔告诉布林肯，哈马斯对以色列、美国、埃及和卡塔尔共同起草的绑架交易作出了回应。据卡塔尔认为，这是一次“积极”的回应，并被布林肯视为有“希望”（尽管也有一些“无法启动的事物”）。然而，内塔尼亚胡总理则将其斥为“荒谬的”。


<div><div><div id="econ-2"></div></div></div><p>The sticking point is whether the fighting will continue after the pause, as Israel wants. Hamas insists on an eventual permanent ceasefire and Israel’s withdrawal from Gaza. The likeliest compromise is a deal that unfolds in phases. America’s hope is that even a temporary pause will change the mindset of both sides, allowing them to consider the “day after”.</p>

关键问题是休战后战斗是否会继续，以色列希望如此。哈马斯坚持最终实现永久停火以及以色列从加沙撤军。最有可能的妥协是逐步实施的协议。美国寄希望于即使暂时的休战也会改变双方的心态，使他们能够考虑“第二天”。


<p>This turns the spotlight on Mr Netanyahu, who has declared his intention to fight for “absolute victory” and his opposition to a Palestinian state. “The day after is the day after Hamas. All of Hamas,” he said. Arab leaders want America to exert more pressure on him. The Biden administration thinks that halting the flow of weapons to Israel would encourage Hamas and the rest of Iran’s “axis of resistance”. Instead Mr Blinken chastised Israel for a death toll that was “too high”, pushed for more humanitarian aid and insisted that it “put civilians first and foremost in mind”. On February 1st America also imposed sanctions on four Jewish settlers accused of violence against Palestinians, irking Mr Netanyahu.</p>

这将聚光灯对内塔尼亚胡，他已经宣布打算为“绝对胜利”而战，并反对巴勒斯坦国。他说：“第二天就是哈马斯的第二天。全部的哈马斯。”阿拉伯领导人希望美国对他施加更多压力。拜登政府认为，停止向以色列提供武器会鼓励哈马斯以及伊朗“抵抗轴心”的其余部分。相反，布林肯指责以色列造成了“伤亡人数过高”，推动了更多的人道主义援助，并坚持认为它“将平民放在首位和首要考虑”。2月1日，美国还对四名被指控暴力对待巴勒斯坦人的犹太定居者实施了制裁，这让内塔尼亚胡感到不悦。


<p>Mr Blinken thinks the region is at a fork. One way lies salvation, with a “future for the better for Israelis, for Arabs, for Palestinians”. The other way leads to damnation, with “an endless cycle of violence and destruction and despair”. Mr Blinken also seems worried about Israeli forces pushing on to Rafah at the southern end of Gaza. Palestinians are increasingly concentrated there and the risk is of their being pushed across the border into Sinai. Seeking to reassure Egypt’s president, Abdel-Fattah al-Sisi, Mr Blinken earlier expressed America’s “rejection of any forced displacement of Palestinians from Gaza”.</p>

布林肯先生认为该地区正处于十字路口。一条路通向救赎，有一个“对以色列人、阿拉伯人和巴勒斯坦人的更好未来”的愿景。另一条路则导向毁灭，伴随着“无尽的暴力、破坏和绝望循环”。

布林肯先生似乎也对加沙南部边缘拉法地区的以色列军队继续推进表示担忧。那里的巴勒斯坦人越来越集中，风险在于他们可能被推过边境进入西奈。

为了安抚埃及总统阿卜杜勒-法塔赫·塞西，布林肯早些时候已经表达了美国“反对在加沙任何强制性驱逐巴勒斯坦人”的立场。


<p>America wants Israel to agree to “a practical, timebound, irreversible path to a Palestinian state” as part of an agreement between America, Israel, the <small>pa</small> and Saudi Arabia. America would offer a defence treaty with Saudi Arabia and civilian nuclear technology. The <small>pa</small> would agree to reform.</p>

美国希望以色列能在美、以、巴和沙特阿拉伯之间的协议中，同意“一个实际的、有时限的、不可逆转的道路，通往巴勒斯坦国”。美国会提供与沙特阿拉伯的安全条约以及民用核技术作为支持。巴勒斯坦方面会承诺改革。


<p>To sweeten the deal, Mr Blinken said, Arab states were offering Israel unspecified “security guarantees’‘. These are unlikely to involve a formal defence treaty; Gulf states do not have big armies, nor do they want to be at the forefront of an American-Israel confrontation with Iran. But informed sources suggest options might include more intelligence-sharing, a common air-defence zone and joint military exercises. Some of this already takes place, but the aim would be to make it more visible and institutionalised. Mr Blinken said Arab states were ready “to do things with and for Israel that they were never prepared to do in the past”; America, too, would agree to do the same.</p>

为促进这一交易，布林肯先生说阿拉伯国家愿意向以色列提供“未公开的安全保证”。这些不太可能涉及正式的国防条约；海湾国家没有庞大的军队，也不想在一场美国和以色列与伊朗对抗中成为前沿。但知情人士暗示，选项可能包括更多的情报共享、共同的防空区以及联合军事演习。其中一些活动已经在进行，但目标是使其更显眼，并制度化。布林肯先生说阿拉伯国家已经准备好“为以色列做一些在过去从未准备做的事情”；美国也会同意做同样的事情。


<p>Moreover, Arab states seem ready to help the <small>pa</small> reform. Foreign ministers from Saudi Arabia, the United Arab Emirates (<small>uae</small>), Qatar, Egypt and Jordan are set to meet <small>pa</small> officials in Riyadh on February 8th to discuss governance. Some Arab sources suggest Jordan could help train Palestinian security forces, and the <small>uae</small> could help improve the <small>pa</small>’s administration.</p>

此外，阿拉伯国家似乎愿意支持巴勒斯坦的改革。来自沙特阿拉伯、阿联酋（UAE）、卡塔尔、埃及和约旦的外交部长们计划于2月8日在利雅得与巴方官员会面，讨论治理问题。一些阿拉伯消息来源暗示，约旦可能帮助训练巴勒斯坦安全部队，而阿联酋可能会帮助改善巴勒斯坦的行政管理。


<p>Arab states have made clear they will not send peacekeepers to Gaza if and when the Israelis leave; nor will they pay to rebuild it unless there is an Israeli commitment to Palestinian statehood. Nevertheless, they seem to understand that they must take greater charge of settling the question of Palestine, or risk Iran and other radicals exploiting it to their advantage.</p>

阿拉伯国家已明确表示，只要以色列人撤离加沙，它们就不会派维和人员去；除非得到以色列关于巴勒斯坦国地位的承诺，否则它们也不会掏钱重建加沙。尽管如此，它们似乎明白必须在解决巴勒斯坦问题上发挥更大作用，否则将冒着伊朗和其他激进势力利用这一问题为自己谋取利益的风险。


<p>In private Mr Netanyahu is said to be more flexible than he lets on. Can he bring himself to say yes to the Saudis? And if he refuses, would those who replace him be more willing? Neither Mr Blinken nor anyone else is certain. <span>■</span></p>

私下里，据说内塔尼亚胡比他在公开场合展现的更加灵活。他能下定决心对沙特人说“是”吗？如果他拒绝了，取代他的那些人会更愿意接受吗？布林肯先生，或者任何人，都无法确定答案。■
